# Next Gen TOM Cadence and Benefits Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines the management rhythm and benefits-credit discipline for the Next Gen operating model.

## Core cadence model

### Engage
- weekly demand triage
- biweekly release and product intake
- monthly portfolio review
- quarterly strategy and workforce review

### Orchestrate
- weekly architecture and release-methods review
- sprint/release cadence for testing and deployment
- monthly standards lifecycle review

### Provision
- daily provisioning watch
- weekly provider and capacity review
- monthly resilience and platform service review
- quarterly skills review

### Service
- daily incident and operations watch
- weekly problem and service review
- monthly SLM, vendor, performance, and continuity review
- quarterly resilience exercise

### Control
- weekly exception and transformation watch
- monthly cost, risk, and control review
- quarterly audit-readiness review
- annual formal attestation where required

## KPI layers

| Layer | Example KPIs |
|---|---|
| TOM | provisioning lead time, automated deployment rate, policy-by-default coverage |
| Business Unit | demand-to-commit lead time, service portfolio throughput |
| Customer Engagement | service satisfaction, first-contact resolution, feature adoption |
| Journey | onboarding completion time, abandonment rate, straight-through processing rate, journey uptime |

## Benefits-credit rule

The benchmark percentages used in planning are heuristic caps, not final forecasts. Benefit credit is only valid when the automated path has displaced the manual path and the change is visible in telemetry, registration, evidence, and cost allocation.

Minimum gates:
1. request flow uses governed catalog/API path
2. standard blueprint exists
3. manual approvals are removed or materially reduced
4. instances auto-register into inventory
5. evidence is captured automatically
6. old manual routing is retired or explicitly capped
