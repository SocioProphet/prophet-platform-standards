# Next Gen TOM Reference

This document is the vendor-neutral reference for the Next Gen operating model.

## Canonical statement

The Next Gen TOM is a **brokerage-centered, hybrid-cloud, ITaaS-style operating model** that organizes technology through five structural domains:
- Engage
- Orchestrate
- Provision
- Service
- Control

It exists to standardize service consumption, automated fulfillment, centralized control transparency, and measurable service economics.

## Operating shape

- **Engage**: primarily federated into business towers and service-consumption fronts
- **Orchestrate**: mixed; central standards plus federated execution
- **Provision**: primarily centralized as the shared brokerage and fulfillment factory
- **Service**: mixed; central operational disciplines with federated service ownership
- **Control**: centralized authority for finance, security, audit, enterprise architecture, sourcing, HR, and PMO/change

## Governance bodies

| Body | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Technology Portfolio Council | demand and portfolio steering |
| Architecture & Brokerage Board | architecture patterns, service blueprints, provider integration patterns |
| Technology Control Council | policy authority, economics, exceptions, audit, security, sourcing, and EA governance |

## Design rules

1. No provisioning without a blueprint.
2. No blueprint without policy attachment.
3. No live service without owner, registration, and observability.
4. No exception without owner, rationale, expiry, and compensating controls.
5. No economic credit unless the automated path materially displaces the manual path.

## Separation of concerns

This standards repository defines the normative operating model. Runtime repositories carry implementation bindings, APIs, schemas, examples, and validation helpers that realize the model in code and deployment topologies.
